## 01  Executive Summary

Vsite is an AI-powered digital menu and ordering platform built for restaurants in South India — starting with Tamil Nadu. The product converts a photo of any paper menu into a live, professional digital storefront in under 3 minutes, at a fraction of what competitors charge.

This document is the complete 2026 playbook to make vsite.in the #1 result across every surface — Google organic search (SEO), AI-generated answers in ChatGPT / Perplexity / Gemini (GEO), and featured snippets / People-Also-Ask boxes (AEO). The strategy is built around three unfair advantages vsite already holds:

- **Speed advantage** — "live in 3 minutes" is a direct, testable, ownable claim.
- **Price advantage** — ₹399/month vs competitors at ₹1,000–₹10,000+/year.
- **Locality advantage** — purpose-built for Tamil Nadu restaurants, their language, and their context.

The plan covers keyword architecture, competitor gap analysis, on-page and technical SEO, GEO training signals, AEO schema and Q&A content, site architecture (all required pages), a 90-day content calendar, and measurable KPIs.

## 04  Technical SEO Strategy

Technical SEO forms the foundation. Without it, even the best content cannot rank. These are the highest-priority fixes for vsite.in in 2026.

### 4.1  Critical Infrastructure Fixes (Week 1)

- **`robots.txt`** — Create `/robots.txt` allowing all crawlers. Block `/manage/`, `/api/`, `/onboarding/` from indexing.
- **`sitemap.xml`** — Generate dynamic sitemap listing `/`, `/pricing`, `/about`, `/support`, `/contact`, `/blog/*` and all live `/shop/[slug]` pages.
- **Canonical tags** — Add `rel="canonical"` to every page to prevent duplicate content from query strings.
- **Open Graph tags** — Add `og:title`, `og:description`, `og:image`, `og:url` to every page for social sharing previews (critical for WhatsApp, Instagram shares).
- **Twitter/X Card meta** — Add `twitter:card`, `twitter:title`, `twitter:image` for platform sharing.
- **Favicon & app icons** — Add `favicon.ico`, `apple-touch-icon.png`, and `manifest.json` for branded browser tabs and PWA.

### 4.3  Core Web Vitals & Performance

Google uses Core Web Vitals as a ranking factor. Target scores: **LCP < 2.5s**, **FID < 100ms**, **CLS < 0.1**.

- **Images** — All `/public/` images must have explicit `width` and `height` to prevent CLS. Use Next.js `<Image>` with `priority` on hero image.
- **Fonts** — Google Fonts via `googleapis.com` loads synchronously in `<head>`. Move to `next/font` (already partially done) and remove the googleapis stylesheet link.
- **Video** — `customer-flow.mp4` should be lazy-loaded. Add `loading="lazy"` and `preload="none"` attributes.
- **Bundle size** — Audit with `next build`. Code-split heavy components (Pricing, FAQ) with dynamic imports.

## 07  GEO — Generative Engine Optimisation

GEO is the discipline of making your brand visible inside AI-generated answers from ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google Gemini, Claude, and Copilot. By April 2026, AI Overviews appear in 55%+ of all Google searches. GEO is no longer optional.

### 7.1  How GEO Works (The Mechanics)

Traditional SEO optimises for a crawler that indexes words. GEO optimises for a neural network that predicts the most likely authoritative answer. The key signals are:

- **Citation frequency** — How often is "vsite" mentioned across the web, reviews, forums, and news
- **Structured content** — Content that directly answers questions (definitions, comparisons, numbered steps)
- **Entity clarity** — AI engines need to understand clearly: vsite = digital menu SaaS for Indian restaurants
- **Community signals** — Reddit, Quora, YouTube comments where real users discuss vsite
- **Recency signals** — Dated content (articles with "Updated: April 2026") ranks higher in AI answers

---

### 7.2  GEO Content Rules for vsite

Every piece of content published on vsite.in must follow these GEO rules:

1. **Answer first, explain second** — The first 50 words of every blog post must directly answer the title question. AI engines extract the first direct answer.
2. **Include statistics with sources** — *"57% of companies are increasing QR investment in 2026 (Supercode Research)"* makes content AI-citable.
3. **Use comparison tables** — AI engines love structured comparisons. Every comparison article must have a formatted table.
4. **Define key terms** — Include a "What is X?" definition section on every topic page. This is the exact format AI engines cite.
5. **Author bylines with expertise** — Add author bio boxes: *"Written by [Name], restaurant technology consultant, Tamil Nadu."* E-E-A-T signals matter.
6. **Date every article** — Always show "Published: Month Year | Updated: Month Year" prominently.
7. **Write for conversational queries** — Structure content to answer "How do I...", "What is...", "Which is better..." formats.

## 08  AEO — Answer Engine Optimisation

AEO is the practice of structuring content to be selected as the direct answer in Google's AI Overviews, Featured Snippets, People Also Ask boxes, and voice search results. ChatGPT now reaches 883 million users monthly. Only 20% of SaaS companies have begun implementing AEO — this is vsite's first-mover window.

### 8.1  Featured Snippet Capture Strategy

Google serves featured snippets for 8–12% of all queries. The formula to capture them:

1. Identify a question keyword vsite wants to own (e.g., *"How much does a digital menu cost in India?"*).
2. Write a blog post with that exact question as the H2 heading.
3. Under that H2, write a **40–60 word direct answer** in plain language.
4. Follow it with supporting detail, a table, or a numbered list.
5. Mark up the page with `FAQPage` or `HowTo` schema.

**Example target snippet:**

> **Q: How much does a digital menu cost for a restaurant in India?**
>
> A digital menu for a restaurant in India costs between ₹399 and ₹1,500 per month depending on the platform and features. vsite offers a QR menu plan at ₹399/month with a one-time setup fee of ₹1,999, making it one of the most affordable options in 2026.

---

### 8.2  People Also Ask (PAA) Domination

PAA boxes appear in 80%+ of Google searches and are sourced from FAQ schema. This is the easiest high-visibility win available. Every answer must be 40–60 words, written in plain language, structured as a direct response.

Priority PAA questions to create content for:

- **"What is a QR code menu for restaurants?"** — Define clearly, mention vsite as an example.
- **"How long does it take to set up a digital menu?"** — Answer: "3 minutes with vsite's AI."
- **"Do customers need an app to use a QR menu?"** — Answer: "No — vsite menus open in any browser."
- **"Is QR menu better than paper menu?"** — Answer with data: cost comparison, speed, hygiene.
- **"What is the best restaurant software in Tamil Nadu?"** — Feature vsite prominently.

---

### 8.3  Voice Search Optimisation

Voice search queries are 29 words on average vs 3 words for typed searches. They are conversational and question-based. Optimise for:

- "Hey Google, what is the best digital menu app for my restaurant?"
- "OK Google, how do I create a QR code menu for my cafe?"
- "Alexa, which restaurant software works in Tamil Nadu?"

**Tactics:** Use conversational language in content. Target question keywords. Add `speakable` schema to FAQ pages. Ensure `LocalBusiness` schema includes phone, address, and area served.
